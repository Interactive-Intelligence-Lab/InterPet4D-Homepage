InterPet4D is the first large-scale multimodal 4D dataset of natural human–dog interactions, with synchronized multi-view + egocentric video, audio, and 3D motion of both humans and dogs.

Abstract

Human–pet interaction estimation and generation remain underexplored due to the absence of high-quality large-scale datasets. We present InterPet4D, the first multimodal dataset capturing natural interactions between humans and dogs. Using a synchronized multi-view capture system, we record human–dog obedience tasks and provide annotations for both humans and dogs, including multiview and egocentric videos, segmentations, 2D/3D keypoints, meshes, and audio tracks. InterPet4D consists of 6.8 million frames collected from 13 dogs of 11 breeds interacting with 23 human participants.

We further introduce the InterPetMoGen framework for human-pet interaction motion generation. Our proposed model achieves an FID score of 11.21, greatly outperforming Seq2Seq or DiT baselines, demonstrating the effectiveness of InterPet4D for modeling realistic human–pet interactions.

Interaction Taxonomy

Four categories of common human–dog interactions, each emphasizing different communication modalities.

🤚 Petting

Close-range hand motion and the dog's postural response (leaning in, tail wagging).

🗣️ Commanding

Verbal + gestural commands (sit, stay, shake, turn around) with the dog's trained responses.

📣 Calling

Calling the dog from a distance using voice and hand beckoning, capturing locomotion and attention shifts.

🎾 Free-form

Unscripted interactions: fetch, tug-of-war, chase — the full diversity of everyday dynamics.

InterPetMoGen (IPMG)

A MotionGPT-style autoregressive framework for gesture-to-pet motion generation.

Figure 3. Human body / hand gestures and audio are encoded into discrete tokens; a transformer with Modality-aware Attention (MMA) masks autoregressively generates dog motion tokens decoded by PetVAE.

  • PetVAE tokenizer — learns a compact discrete latent for dog motion.
  • Modality-specific encoders for body, hand, translation, and audio.
  • Modality-aware Attention (MMA) masks — combine bidirectional conditioning with causal autoregressive decoding for coarse-to-fine motion generation.
